# Three openers — with exact scripts

**1. The "call me in 6 months" reversal**
> "Sorry Luis, you asked us to reach back out back in Q1… And I think we might have dropped the ball here. Our bad."
Uses a previous interaction to reframe the call as a follow-through, not an interruption.

**2. Permission-based opener**
> "Nick, you're probably gonna hate me for this, but it's a cold call. Can I get 27 seconds to tell you why I'm calling… Then you can hang up on me afterwards?"
Transparency about the call type pre-empts the "I'm busy" brush-off. The 27-second specificity is intentional — sounds more credible than a round number.

**3. Social proof / "heard the name tossed around"**
> "Hey Jane, we work with a few other Sequoia portfolio companies. It's Armand at Pave. Heard our name tossed around?"
Leads with relevance through peer company relationships before introducing yourself — reverses the standard opener structure.

**Significance:** Farrokh is VP Sales-turned-operator, not a trainer who never carried a bag. All three openers come from his own production call experience at Pave and Carta, not from a textbook. The 396-reaction engagement indicates these resonated as recognizably battle-tested, not theoretical.
