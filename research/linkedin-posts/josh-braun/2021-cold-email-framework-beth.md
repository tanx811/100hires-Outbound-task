# Cold email framework: Person → Problem → Solution

Josh Braun walks through building a cold email live, using a hypothetical prospect "Beth," an Executive Director of Human Services who manages payroll and benefits.

**Framework — three inputs before writing a word:**
1. **The Person** — Beth, with a specific job responsibility.
2. **The Problem** — "Labor intensive process, 10+ hours a month using a calculator."
3. **The Solution** — "Run payroll in 90 seconds."

**Personalization layer:** research the prospect's LinkedIn for a personal detail. Braun found Beth runs 5Ks, and used it in both subject line and sign-off.

**Resulting email:**
> Subject: Beth, does running payroll take longer than running a 5k?
> Moss is running payroll in 90 seconds — syncing HR data with payroll. Want to learn how? Regardless, rock your runs.

Braun calls this style "ETLLAT" — Email That Looks Like A Text — deliberately informal and conversational rather than traditional sales copy.
