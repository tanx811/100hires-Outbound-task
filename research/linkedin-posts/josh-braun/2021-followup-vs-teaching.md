# "Outteach your competition" — TOM sequences beat check-ins

Braun contrasts two sales training companies after a stalled deal (lost on timing, not price):

- **Company A:** Calls quarterly to ask "has timing improved?"
- **Company B:** Sends a monthly video with one practical cold-email tip the prospect can use immediately.

> "Teaching forges a strong bond with people. Teaching creates trust. Checking in erodes trust."

**Recommended structure — Top of Mind (TOM) sequence:**
- Build a series of emails, one per common customer question (he uses seven).
- Distribute the same content across LinkedIn and YouTube, not just email.
- Enroll stalled prospects into the sequence instead of manually checking in.
- The payoff is staying useful — not present — until timing changes.

Closing line: "Outteach your competition."
