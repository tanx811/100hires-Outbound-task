# The subject line is the gatekeeper — four that actually work

> "You can spend countless hours writing the perfect cold email, but if you don't know how to write a good subject line… NOBODY is going to open your email and read it."

Dang ran his own experiment: built large prospect lists, tested every subject line variant he could think of, tracked open rates across thousands of sends. Result: four formulas that consistently outperformed everything else (detailed in the linked YouTube video).

**What we know from the post:**
- He identified four "bulletproof" subject line patterns through empirical testing, not theory
- The shared principle: subject lines should not give away the full pitch — they create just enough curiosity to earn the open
- Target outcome: "start getting meetings with your dream clients"

**Why included:** Dang is one of the few cold-email creators who shows the test-and-iterate methodology behind his advice rather than just asserting "this works." The same empiricism shows up in his YouTube course content (see `research/youtube-transcripts/patrick-dang/`).
