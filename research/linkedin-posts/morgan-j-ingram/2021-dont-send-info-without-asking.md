# Never send information without asking what they want to see

> "One of the worst things we can do is to send information and [assume] the prospect knows what they want."

Ingram's fix: before sending anything, ask "What type of information would you like to see?" This turns a generic info-dump into a *give/get* scenario — you get a signal of intent, they get targeted materials that match their actual question.

**Why it works:** A prospect who tells you what they want to see has implicitly agreed the topic is relevant to them. Closing from that position is far easier than following up on an email nobody asked for. Commenters confirmed: this technique "kept the conversation going" and surfaced more buying signals than ending the call and going dark.

**Cross-reference:** pairs directly with Braun's "Diffuse → Discuss → Discover" framework (`research/linkedin-posts/josh-braun/`) — both approaches use questions to disqualify irrelevant pitches early rather than assuming fit.
