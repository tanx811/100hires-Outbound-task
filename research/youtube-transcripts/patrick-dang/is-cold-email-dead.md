# "Is Cold Email Dead?" — no, the methodology is the bottleneck

Dang's argument: cold email is "alive and thriving," and people who think otherwise are blaming the channel for their own lack of methodology.

**Supporting data he cites:**
- ~3 billion email users worldwide (2019 baseline, still growing)
- 58% of adults check email immediately on waking
- ~4 hours/week average time spent on email

**Why email specifically, as a channel:**
- It's the backbone infrastructure every other channel (social platforms included) still depends on for account recovery/identity.
- Sending cost is effectively zero — scaling to 100+ sends/day costs nothing incremental.

**Conclusion:** the combination of massive reach + high daily engagement + zero marginal cost makes email worth mastering properly rather than abandoning for the next channel fad — a useful counterweight to Sam Dunning's 2026 "cold outreach is failing" position elsewhere in this research set.
